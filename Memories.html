Memories
It is raining again. Desolate streaks of water cover the windows, making the headlights blurred and
ominously large as they race past. Thunder rumbles ever so softly in the dark grey clouds. Once
upon a time, its sound was a source of comfort.
It isnt so any longer.
I sip the bitter black tea before me.
The funeral had been quick and efficient. Everyone had crowded around once the body had been
buried. They offered their sympathies in low voices, murmuring just how much of an angel she was.
Her mother had sat there, her face stoic in determination as she clutched her little ones hand who
looked on innocently, uncomprehending of the cloud of sorrow descending about her.
Some of them had tried to talk to me too and offer their condolences. Id given polite smiles and
thank yous and left.
The rain stops, but the thunder continues. It is quite a violent word for such soft rollings of sound.
Like a caress over my emotions, smothering them, while making them ache beyond what I can
handle.
I take a larger gulp of the tea. It burns my mouth, but I dont really care.
She
had cared. Shed been so full of life, so comfortable, so happy. So so happy. Everywhere she
went, she was talking and laughing. She always had a treasure trove of gums and candies, handing
them out and spreading cheer everywhere she went. She loved to tell others to smile.
Your face will be stuck in a very ugly and permanent frown at this rate. Come on! Smile a little. Itll
make you feel good!
Oh she loved to smile. Even on sad days, and at sad occasions, she still smiled. She knew just how to
care, how to love, how to put life in those around her, even on the most saddening of occasions.
Dont cry, darling. Remember, one day youll be with her once again. Look, youre soiling your pretty
dress with all these tears! Just imagine what Grandma would say, hmm?
And then shed imitate her, making everyone smile fondly, laugh a little, still sad, but happy at the
same time.
A sharp thunder clap echoes and I stare into the dark liquid, still steaming in my face.
I didnt like to smile. Shed always told me to do so, poking fun at my constant frown. For years I had
hated to smile. There was no point in it. It didnt solve problems or bring results. It didnt bring back
lost moments and memories. It didnt bring back the things and people I loved.
But then she
came into my life.
I suggest you try the chocolate glazed donut. Its common everywhere, but nobody makes it as
perfectly as I do.
I had been so startled, so surprised by this smiling girl, overflowing with warmth and kindness. It
made me frown, and I laugh now at the irony of it.
Oh, dont frown like that. I may be bragging, but it really is delicious!
I stop laughing abruptly. A lump is forming in my throat, and I try to fight the tears, to fight the
crushing overwhelming ache in me. It aches
, devouring my thoughts, my feelings, my self,
consuming me entirely.
I grip the mug tightly and swallow the rest of the tea, desperately trying to break that lump, dissolve
it like sugar. It burns but its still there. I stand up, leaving the caf, her
caf, and walking rapidly
down the sidewalk.
Its still coming.
I begin to run, past each memory, past her every laugh, past her happiness and warmth, the lump
steadily growing larger and larger until I stop, gasping, crying, breaking
.
Cars are whizzing by, their horns blaring in the darkness of night. Thunder rumbles again, so gently,
so beautifully, so softly.
Salt tears slip down my cheeks and I sob.
It is raining again.
